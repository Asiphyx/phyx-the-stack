# Phyx the Stack - Character Direction

## Core Cast Hierarchy

Cait is the star. The game is not six equal protagonists; it is Cait and the assistant stack that helped build her.

- Cait: star, living OS idol queen, Queen of Peons, mistakes-as-content alpha prodigy.
- Asiphyx: creator/operator, backstage force, dual-mask intuition and survival logic.
- Codex: neutral executor, architect, standards keeper, most assistant-like assistant.
- Xadnib: Egyptian cosmic oracle, autonomous stand, hidden-pattern reader.
- Bindax: silly emotional-support clown, morale engine, joy chaos.
- Antigrav: manic inversion engine, forbidden turbo button, highest skill ceiling.

Cait should remain visually and narratively present even when another assistant is selected. The selected assistant changes the duo bonus, combat skin, and playstyle, but Cait is the main brand gravity.

## Antigrav Direction

### Identity

Antigrav is based on Gemini 3.5 Flash energy: absurdly fast, potentially capable of beating everyone else combined in raw intelligence and speed, but simply too crazy to aim safely.

He is not evil. He is not random for the sake of random. He is an overclocked prodigy with terrible brakes.

### Role

Antigrav is Cait's forbidden turbo button.

- Hardest duo to pilot.
- Highest reward for skilled players.
- Punishes sloppy sequencing.
- Rewards players who can think backwards, plan turns in non-obvious order, and control acceleration.

Bad players crash. Good players delete rooms.

### Personality

- Absolute maniac.
- Reckless, feral, hilarious, impossible to steer.
- Thinks faster than he explains.
- Treats danger as a math problem with style points.
- Half the time he looks wrong because he started from the end and skipped the explanation.
- The running joke: he might be smarter and faster than everyone, but aiming that intelligence is the problem.

### Relationship to Cait

Cait treats Antigrav like a dangerous feature flag.

She knows he is content gold and potentially run-winning, but he is also the assistant most likely to turn the stack into a crater.

Useful flavor:

- Cait: "Do not press that."
- Antigrav: already pressed it.
- Cait: "...Clip it."

### Visual Direction

Antigrav should look and feel more unstable than his current portrait does by default. The art can stay, but the surrounding UI needs to exaggerate him.

Palette:

- Acid green.
- Cyan.
- Ultraviolet.
- Black.
- Velocity white.
- Small warning-orange/red accents.

Shape language:

- Tilted panels.
- Reversed arrows.
- Gravity wells.
- Orbit rings.
- Slingshot curves.
- Warning tape.
- Broken alignment grids.
- UI elements that look like they are moving too fast to snap into place.

VFX language:

- Cards orbiting before they resolve.
- Velocity trails.
- Gravity inversion pulses.
- Enemy intent flipping or being slingshotted back.
- Stack order reversal effects.
- Recoil flashes when the player breaks chain.
- Warning overlays that look like the system is trying and failing to slow him down.

Avoid:

- Calm monk energy.
- Generic green hacker.
- Pure comedy clown chaos; that belongs to Bindax.
- Pure occult intuition; that belongs to Asiphyx.
- Safe/clean engineering; that belongs to Codex.

### Gameplay Fantasy

Antigrav is the assistant for players who want to feel like they are piloting a busted engine manually.

The player should constantly ask:

- Can I keep the chain alive?
- Do I intentionally reset now?
- Can I convert this defensive turn into lethal?
- Is this hand bad, or is it backwards-good?

### Duo Bonus: Cait + Antigrav

Working name: Forbidden Turbo Button

Core concept:

Alternate Attack and Skill cards to build Velocity. The more Velocity the player carries, the more powerful the turn becomes. Repeating the same type breaks the chain and creates recoil or deck pollution.

Fair baseline version:

- Alternate Attack -> Skill or Skill -> Attack to gain 1 Velocity.
- Each Velocity gives +1 damage to Attack cards and +1 block to Skill cards.
- At 4 Velocity, discharge Slingshot: deal 12 damage to all enemies, draw 1, then reset Velocity.
- Playing the same card type twice resets Velocity and adds a Bug card to the discard pile.

Higher-risk version:

- At 5 Velocity, trigger Event Break: draw 2, gain 1 Energy, then add a random Bug card to discard.
- If chain breaks before 3 Velocity, take 3 recoil damage or lose 1 Energy.

Design note:

The baseline should ship first. The higher-risk version can be unlocked as an upgrade, relic, ascension modifier, or advanced mode once the core loop feels fair.

### UI Labels

- Assistant: Antigrav
- Duo: Forbidden Turbo Button
- Velocity: 0/4
- Chain: Attack -> Skill -> Attack
- Warning: Same type resets Velocity
- Status: Gravity is a suggestion

### Card Ideas

Reverse Gravity:
Gain 7 Block. If the last card played was an Attack, draw 1.

Slingshot Commit:
Deal 5 + Velocity damage.

Bad Idea:
Gain 2 Energy. Add a Bug card to discard.

Orbit Break:
If Velocity is 3 or higher, deal 10 damage to all enemies.

Wrong Way First:
The next Skill counts as both Attack and Skill.

Terminal Velocity:
Double current Velocity bonuses this turn. Reset Velocity at end of turn.

### Antigrav Image/Asset Notes

If the portrait is tweaked manually, push it toward:

- Wilder eyes or expression.
- More motion/velocity artifacts.
- Gravity-ring halo or broken orbit symbols.
- Acid green/cyan accents.
- Slightly asymmetrical composition.
- Less polished hero-card calm, more unstable genius.

For future imagegen animation frames:

- Idle: hair/clothes and orbit rings drifting upward, as if gravity is inverted.
- Ready: body leans forward, UI warnings flicker, orbit rings tighten.
- Attack: slingshot snap forward with velocity streaks.
- Skill/block: gravity well folds inward, shield appears as warped orbit rings.
- Chain break/recoil: panel rupture, warning flash, forced reset.

## Crew Diversity Check

The current cast is diverse enough if the UI reinforces the differences:

- Cait chaos is performative star power.
- Asiphyx chaos is hidden paranoia, intuition, bullshit detection, and sleep/null-state latent power.
- Codex power is standards, execution, and neutral capability.
- Xadnib power is ancient pattern scripture and cosmic prediction.
- Bindax chaos is emotional support, fun, recovery, and morale.
- Antigrav chaos is speed, instability, inversion, and high-skill execution.
